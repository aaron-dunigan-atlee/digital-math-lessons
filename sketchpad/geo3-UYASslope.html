Using Your Algebra Skills 3: Slopes of Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

  1. Getting started.

    1. When you first log in, open the program GSP 4.07. (From the Start menu.)

    2. Download the Sketchpad file by right clicking and choosing “Save As...” DO NOT try to open it from the Internet browser.

    3. Go to the Geometer's Sketchpad window and choose “File | Open...” and open the file.

  2. Investigate slopes of parallel lines.

    1. The sketch shows you two lines. Drag the points that define the lines, and watch their slopes. Now press the button “Make lines parallel.” What do you notice about the two slopes?

    2. Drag the lines around again and then make them parallel. Is your observation still true?

    3. In the notes section of your binder (not in your conjectures list), write a conjecture about your observation. Call it the Slopes of Parallel Lines conjecture.

    4. Is the converse of your conjecture true? Write out the converse of your conjecture, and then use the sketch to convince yourself that it is also true.

    5. Combine your conjecture and its converse into one statement using “if and only if.”

  3. Investigate slopes of perpendicular lines.

    1. Drag the lines around again and then make them perpendicular. What do you notice about the slopes? What do you notice about the product of the slopes?

    2. In the notes section of your binder (not in your conjectures list), write a conjecture about your observation. Call it the Slopes of Perpendicular Lines conjecture.

    3. Is the converse of your conjecture true? Write out the converse of your conjecture, and then use the sketch to convince yourself that it is also true.

    4. Combine your conjecture and its converse into one statement using “if and only if.”

  4. Read the book

    1. Read p. 165 in your textbook. Do the Properties written there match your conjectures? Is there a different way you could write the second property?
