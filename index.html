Amish Aaron's GeoGebra Activities

Some activities showing what can be done wiht GeoGebra to enhance high-school math instruction.

Horizontal Translations

Sketch

Instructions

A sketch for demonstrating why the graph of y=f(x+k) translates in the opposite direction to the sign of k.

Derivatives Roller Coaster

Sketch

Instructions

Students learn intuitive concepts about derivatives by thinking about the direction and speed of a roller coaster that rides along the function's graph. "If you can imagine riding a roller coaster, you can understand calculus."

Introduction to Exponents

No Sketch

Instructions

An investigative approach to introducing exponential functions (okay, I know DVD's are kind of old school--I wrote this a while ago; it needs some updating).

Introduction to Sinusoidal Functions

No Sketch

Instructions

Students discover the shape and properties of sinusoidal functions by plotting periodic data related to sunrise times.

Investigate Graphs of Sinusoidal Functions

Sketch

Instructions

Students explore the impact of the parameters on the shape of sine and cosine graphs.

Investigate Sound Waves

Sketch

Instructions

After a unit about sine and cosine graphs, students apply their knowledge to analyze the graph of a sound wave generated in class with a tuning fork. Students use the results to determine if the tuning fork is in tune. The GeoGebra sketch shows a sample result.
